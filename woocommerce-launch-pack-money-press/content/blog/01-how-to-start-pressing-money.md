# How to Start Pressing Money: The First 3 Moves That Actually Matter

Most people overthink the start. They read 12 books, follow 40 finance accounts, and still don't do anything. Here's the actual order of operations for someone starting from zero.

## Move 1: Know What You're Working With

You can't press money you haven't accounted for yet. Before anything else, spend 20 minutes mapping out where your money actually goes each month — not where you think it goes. Income in, every expense out, what's left. Even if the number is ugly, you need it. The Money Press Budget Tracker was built specifically for this step.

## Move 2: Build One Month of Expenses as a Buffer

Before you invest anything, before you save aggressively, before you do anything aggressive — build one month of living expenses in a separate account. This isn't your emergency fund yet. It's just a buffer so an unexpected $400 expense doesn't destroy your momentum.

## Move 3: Automate One Savings Action

After the buffer is built, set up one automatic transfer on payday. Even $50. The automation matters more than the amount. Once you automate it, it's no longer a decision — it's a system. Systems compound. Decisions drain you.

These three moves don't require a financial advisor, a high income, or a perfect credit score. They require a spreadsheet, a separate account, and a scheduled transfer.

Start there. The rest builds on top.
