# 5 High-ROI Habits That Cost Almost Nothing

The internet sells complicated. The reality is most of the highest-ROI moves in personal finance and productivity are boring, simple, and free.

## 1. Weekly Money Review (15 Minutes)

Once a week, same day and time, look at your account balances and your budget tracker. That's it. You're not analyzing, you're just looking. The act of looking keeps you honest and prevents the 3-month drift where you don't check anything and end up $800 off-plan.

## 2. 24-Hour Rule on Non-Essential Purchases

Before any purchase over $50 that isn't a necessity — wait 24 hours. Put it in the cart. Close the tab. Come back tomorrow. You'll buy about 60% of what you would have bought same-session. The other 40% was just the dopamine talking.

## 3. Track Net Worth Monthly

One number. Assets minus liabilities. Write it down in your journal or spreadsheet. Watch it move. The number going up by $200 in a month doesn't feel exciting until you've tracked it for 6 months and watched the line move. Net worth tracking turns abstract financial goals into something measurable.

## 4. Read One Financial Concept Per Week

Not books — concepts. "What is dollar-cost averaging?" "What is a Roth IRA?" "What is an expense ratio?" One concept per week, 10 minutes of reading. In six months you'll have more working knowledge than most people who've "been adulting" for a decade.

## 5. Stack Two Automation Tasks

Automate your savings transfer. Automate your investing contribution (even $25/month into an index fund to start). Two automations running in the background mean two decisions you no longer have to make consciously every month. Those decisions accumulate into a system.
